About Astana

Astana, the capital city of Kazakhstan, is a modern and vibrant metropolis with impressive architecture, rich history, and cultural significance. Known for its futuristic skyline and as a hub for political and economic activity, Astana is a key destination in Kazakhstan.

  • Population: 1.2 million
  • Founded: 1830
  • Main Language: Kazakh, Russian
  • Climate: Continental, cold winters and warm summers
  • Postal code: 010000
  • Automobile code: 01
  • Time Zone: UTC +5

Astana is not only the political heart of the country but also a center of innovation and culture. With landmarks such as the Bayterek Tower and the Khan Shatyr Entertainment Center, the city offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity. Tourists can explore its museums, parks, and architectural marvels, while enjoying the city's modern amenities.

Landmarks in Astana

Astana, the modern capital of Kazakhstan, is home to some of the most impressive architectural landmarks in Central Asia. One of the most iconic symbols of the city is the Bayterek Tower, a futuristic structure offering stunning panoramic views of the city from its observation deck. The Palace of Peace and Reconciliation, designed by famed architect Norman Foster, stands as a symbol of unity, featuring a striking glass pyramid. For a blend of tradition and modernity, visit the Hazret Sultan Mosque, one of the largest mosques in Central Asia. Another must-see is Khan Shatyr, a massive tent-shaped structure that houses a shopping center, entertainment facilities, and even an indoor beach. Finally, the Presidential Palace (Ak Orda) showcases the heart of Kazakhstan’s government and offers insight into the country’s leadership.

History of Astana

Astana’s history is a unique blend of tradition and rapid modernization. Originally established in 1830 as a small settlement called Akmolinsk, the city served as a fortress on the northern steppes. It went through several name changes, including Tselinograd during the Soviet era, reflecting its role as a hub for agricultural development.

In 1997, Astana was declared the new capital of Kazakhstan, moving the seat of government from Almaty. Since then, it has undergone rapid development, transforming from a modest provincial town into a vibrant, modern metropolis. In 2019, the city was briefly renamed Nur-Sultan in honor of the country's first president, Nursultan Nazarbayev, but returned to its current name Astana in 2022.

Today, Astana is a symbol of Kazakhstan’s ambitions on the global stage, combining historical roots with cutting-edge architecture and urban design.

Travel Tips for Astana

Astana can be visited year-round, but be prepared for extreme weather conditions. Winters can be harsh, with temperatures dropping as low as -30°C (-22°F), so pack plenty of warm clothing if you're visiting in colder months. Summers are warm and pleasant, making it an ideal time for exploring the city on foot.

When traveling around the city, the public transportation system is reliable, with buses being the main form of transport. Taxis are also readily available but ensure that you agree on the fare before the ride. It’s a good idea to carry cash, as not all places accept credit cards. For those unfamiliar with the language, learning a few basic phrases in Russian or Kazakh can be helpful, though English is increasingly spoken in tourist areas.

Lastly, Astana is a city that blends tradition with modernity, so whether you're visiting its historical landmarks or enjoying its shopping centers, respect local customs while enjoying the city’s diverse culture.
